Fixarr:

Fixarr is a work in progress tool designed to allow anyone to take "broken" music files, with issues like being too loud, quiet and most importantly to me, possesing incorrect, missing, jumbled or misplaced metadata. As a bonus there are plans to (in the far future) have cross device backups, music info browsing, auto-tune, audio ripping from videos and automatic file organisation. Currently the project is at a bit of an uphill because i am being adament about having at least a variety of the program as a LUI, and microsoft just did not design the windows 11 console for this at all. The windows 10 console is much easier to use just because of small differences that stack up, and im not going to require everyone who wants to use the console version to roll their console back to windows 10. I will provide a GUI version after the 1.0, I do want people to actually use it, but just the idea of the possibility of having it as a powershell addon sparks something in me. Either way the documentation for it is right here. I do plan on chucking the documentation onto this site eventually but as you can probably tell when you read it, I used it as an NEA for college, but there are currently higher priorities than reorganizing and reformatting the whole 200 pages. When i submitted it for my NEA it was more like 500 so ive done a lot already.

Radio / Aux / Bluetooth audio car sender:

This is currently very much in the theoretical phase. I just want to make a single device that has capabilities for connecting to any cars speakers. This could be radio, aux or bluetooth depending on the car. It would be pretty simple, just the option of giving it files via usb, micro-sd, aux, bluetooth or internal storage, having those files processed and output via one of the aforementioned channels. The output and input will be changed via 2 independant scroll wheels and a screen / screenss to display the current settings. The screens shouldn't pose a problem for distracting the driver since it is a very set and done thing. I would also have the screens turn off after a couple seconds just to make extra sure that that is the case and save power. An issue posed is how songs are changed and paused. Adding more buttons that are connected wirelessly would needlessly drain power and recharging the buttons would be a pain not even talking about how easy it would be to lose them. a whole wireless pannel would avoid the last two problems but customisability on placement of the buttons would suffer greatly. Keeping the buttons on the device would make it difficult to change and dangerous to do while driving and having the aforementioned panel be attached by a wire would be clunky and inconvinient although it does seem to be my favourite sollution so far and exposed wires are my jam. There are options to integrate other ways of changing the music like if the car has bluetooth and already has media buttons built in that would be nice. Voice controls would be fun.
