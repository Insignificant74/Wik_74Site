Hobbies:

Hiking:

I love walking and climbing through different ranges, i have been all over The UK, from Snowdonia to the Highlands, aswell as Poland, including the tatras and Bieszczady. My greatest achievements in this are probably doing Crib Goch along with Glyder Fawr and Glyder Fach in one go aswell as the hardest routes up Tryfan all in awful conditions, but my truly greatest feat is doing the trail from the sea's eye lake up to the Rysy peak and back, a trail that takes about 7.5 hours total, in around 3.5 hours. I kept getting people talking to me both ways, talking about how they had seen me going up and back down while they had only just started nearing the top.

That was the first time i had been allowed to go off on my own (a decision that my mother regretted soon after my departure and then spent the next 3 hours panicking about) and it felt better than anything else did. After that trial by 14 year old me my current goal has been to complete the eagle's perch trail and even extend it to Świnica. There have been two attempts at this so far. The first ended with my climbing partner chickening out and us taking a side route to get down, the second ended with me getting about 1/3 of the way across after which very shakey and generally weak, which hadnt been too surprising seeing as I didn't bring any food (it was a monday, could not buy anything the previous day (dumb polish laws)). When i did finally get to my hostel it turned out I had a rather bad case of food poisoning but this didn't impact the trip too much since it was raining for the rest of our outing.

This is a photo by piechurek7 on blogger.com. I personally feel that stopping to take images and narrow the experience down to a series of pixels takes away from the experience, and my climbing partner, who does not share that opinion, was too busy shitting themselves to take any. piechurek7 was the only source of images that i found to give the trail any justice (while having no cc license), and messing around with embedding videos and rights to those videos would be too bothersome. You're a capable young person, if you were that interested you could do your own research.

Sports:

Other than Hiking i cycle and walk a considerable ammount. One time I walked from Zamość to Krasnobród in a single day (about 8 hours / 30km). The plan was to continue further on to Józefów, Zwierzyniec and Sczebrzeszyn, throwing down a tent near each one (would've been about 100km total), however after that one day I had gotten rather lost twice, which i will blame on bad trail markings, and along with the intense heat and awfully beaten down shoes, i ended up getting blisters too bad to walk on and found a coach going back to Zamość. I ended up bedridden for a couple of days but I consider this a worthwile wxperience

Cycling wise my most recent ride worth talking about was from Andover to Southampton and back in the same day. The plan was to go to Lyndhurst which would have been about the same distance but I decided that taking a selfie by the water and sending it to my mother would have been much funnier. Southampton however is an absolute mess and actually getting to any water was more difficult than the cycle itself. Did not help that my phone was pretty much dead and that i got distracted and rather lost around the town centre and train tracks. Either way i did actually achieve what i had set out to do for once, The only reprecussion of which was a minor incident the next day of my heart going absolutely balistic and me getting sent home from college but i refuse to accept that those are related.
